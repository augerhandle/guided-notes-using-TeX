\section{Classifying Polynomials}
Polynomials can be classified by the \gap{number} \gap{of} \gap{terms}:
\begin{center}
    \large\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.6}
    \begin{tabular}{|l|l|p{2.25in}|}
        \hline
        \gap[b]{monomial} & \gap{1} term & \\
        \hline
        \gap[b]{binomial} & \gap{2} terms &  \\
        \hline
        \gap[b]{trinomial} & \gap{3} terms & \\ 
        \hline
        \gap[b]{polynomial} & \gap{many} terms & \\
        \hline
    \end{tabular}    
\end{center}

Polynomials can also be {classified} by \gap{degree}:
\begin{center}
    \large\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.6}
    \begin{tabular}{|l|l|p{2.25in}|}
        \hline
        \gap[b]{constant} 
            & polynomial of degree \gap{0}
            & \\
        \hline
        \gap[b]{linear} 
            & polynomial of degree \gap{1}
            &  \\
        \hline
        \gap[b]{quadratic} 
            & polynomial of degree \gap{2}
            &  \\ 
        \hline
        \gap[b]{cubic} 
            & polynomial of degree \gap{3} 
            & \\
        \hline
    \end{tabular}    
\end{center}


Polynomials \gap{never} have variables that

\begin{minipage}{0.49\textwidth}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item have \gap{negative} exponents
        \item are in the \gap{denominator}
    \end{itemize}
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}{0.49\textwidth}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item have \gap{non-integer} exponents
        \item are under a \gap{radical}
    \end{itemize}
\end{minipage}
