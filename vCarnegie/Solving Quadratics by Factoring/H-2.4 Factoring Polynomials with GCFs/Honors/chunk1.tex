\section{Factoring a monomial}

The monomial $24x^5$ can be factored in several ways:

\begin{minipage}{0.49\textwidth}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item $24 \cdot x \cdot x^4 $
    \item $ 3 \cdot 8 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3 $
\end{itemize}
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}{0.49\textwidth}
    \begin{itemize}[nosep]
        \item $ 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 4 \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x^2 $
        \item \fbox{$ 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x \cdot x $}
    \end{itemize}
\end{minipage}
    
\begin{myConceptSteps}{To \gap{completely} factor a monomial\dots}
    [Use a \gap{factor} \gap{tree} in the following steps.]
    \myStep{coefficient}{%
        Rewrite the \myEmph{coefficient} as a product of \gap{prime} \gap{numbers}.
    }
    \myStep{variable}{%
        Expand the variable's exponents and put the \gap{variable} 
        into the factor tree (maybe \gap{several} times).
    }
\end{myConceptSteps}


\myProblems[Completely factor these monomials.]
    {
        $ 60 z^4 $
    }
    {
        $ 18 x^3y^2 $
    }
