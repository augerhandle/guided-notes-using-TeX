\myWideProblemWithContent[%
Use Desmos to find the linear and quadratic regression functions 
that best ``fit'' the data in the given table. 
(After you create the Desmos $x_1-y_1$ table, click the 
magnifying ``Zoom fit'' icon 
\includegraphics[width=0.2in]{magnifying-glass-plus.png}
so that you can see the whole scatterplot.)
]
{
    A 12oz can of soda was put into a freezer.
    This table shows the volume of the soda in the can,
    measured at different temperatures.\\[0.5em]
    \begin{minipage}{0.35\textwidth}
        \footnotesize
        \begin{tabular}{cc}
            $x$ & $y$ \\ 
            temperature (${}^\circ F$) & volume ($cm^3$) \\
            \midrule 
            68.0 & 355.51 \\
            50.0 & 354.98 \\ 
            42.8 & 354.89 \\ 
            39.2 & 354.88 \\ 
            35.6 & 354.89 \\ 
            32.0 & 354.93 \\ 
            23.0 & 355.13 \\ 
            14.0 & 355.54 \\ 
            \bottomrule
        \end{tabular}
        \end{minipage}
    \begin{minipage}{0.64\textwidth}
        \begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,width=\textwidth]
            \small
            \scshape{Linear Regression}\hfill$y_1 \sim m x_1 + b$
            \tcblower
            $m =$ \gap{\tiny$0.000055$} \whenHONORS{\hfill $r^2 = \gap{0.00001}$}\\
            $b =$ \gap{\tiny$355.09$}
        \end{tcolorbox}
        \begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,width=\textwidth]
            \small
            \scshape{Quadratic Regression}\hfill$y_1 \sim A x_1^2 + B x_1 + C$
            \tcblower
            $A =$ \gap{\tiny$0.00088$} \whenHONORS{\hfill $R^2 = \gap{0.9943}$}\\
            $B =$ \gap{\tiny$-0.072$}\\
            $C =$ \gap{\tiny$356.35$}
        \end{tcolorbox}
    \end{minipage}\\[1\onelineskip]
    Click the 
    \includegraphics[width=0.2in]{magnifying-glass-plus.png} 
    icon to make sure you can see all the points.
    Look at the graphs of the two regression equations 
    (line and parabola).
    Which one seems to best fit the points?
    Explain why you say that.
    \vspace{0.7in}
}
