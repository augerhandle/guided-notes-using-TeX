\section*{Problem 1 --- Student Loans}
This table shows average student loan debt 
accumulated by college graduates during years since 2011.
\begin{center}
    % \small
    \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.25}
    \begin{tabular}{c|c|c}
        \toprule
        year 
            & 
                \begin{tabular}{c}
                    $x$\\
                    {\tiny\itshape years since 2011} \\
                \end{tabular}
            & 
            \begin{tabular}{c}
                $y$\\
                {\tiny\itshape loan debt (thousands of \$)}
            \end{tabular}
            \\
        \midrule 
        2011 & \gap{0}  & 12.75 \\
        2015 & \gap{4}  & 17.35 \\
        2019 & \gap{8}  & 18.65 \\ 
        2023 & \gap{12} & 23.20 \\
        2024 & \gap{13} & 24.00 \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}
Use \myDesmos to do a \myEmph{linear} regression on these data.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Fill in the second column ({\itshape years since 2011}).
    \item Enter the $x$ and $y$ data into a \myDesmos $\mathtt{x_1}$--$\mathtt{y_1}$ table.
    \item Do a \myEmph{linear regression} in \myDesmos{}.
    \item Write the regression equation, and find $r^2$.
\end{itemize}
\begin{center}
    \small
    \setlength{\tabcolsep}{1.3em}
    \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
    \begin{tabular}{c|c}
        \toprule
        {\itshape regression equation} & $r^2$ \\
        \midrule 
        $y=$ \gap{0.8303}$x$ + \gap{13.05} & $r^2 =$ \gap{0.974}\\ 
        % $y=$ \gap{0.154}$x^2$ + \gap{-0.0697}$x$ + \gap{47.8} & $r^2 =$ \gap{0.4288}\\
        % $y=$ \gap{aaa}$x^3$ + \gap{bbb}$x^2$ + \gap{ccc}$x$ + \gap{ddd} & $r^2 =$ \gap{xxx} \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}


% \vspace{\onelineskip}
\noindent
If $r^2$ is close to 1.0, the model is a ``good fit'' for the data.
If $r^2$ is close to 0.0, the model is a ``bad fit''.
Is this linear model good or bad? 
Explain your reasoning.\\[0.65\onelineskip]
