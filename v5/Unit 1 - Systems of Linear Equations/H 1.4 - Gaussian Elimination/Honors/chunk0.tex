\section{Augmented Matrices}

An \gap{augmented} \gap{matrix} is a simpler way to write systems of equations.
It has variable \gap{coefficients} in the first several columns 
and the \gap{constant} terms in the last column.

\hfil
{
  \sysdelim..\systeme{
    2x+3y+4z=1,
    5x+6y+7z=2,
    8x+9y+10z=3
}
{
  \LARGE
  $\Longleftrightarrow$
}
{
    \large
  \(
    \begin{bmatrix}[ccc|c]
      2 & 3 & 4 & 1\\
      5 & 6 & 7 & 2\\
      8 & 9 & 10 & 3
    \end{bmatrix}
  \)
}
}
{\scshape Dimensions:} \gap{$3\times4$}
\hfil 

\begin{tcolorbox}[center,width=6.5in]
    \large
    Before you can find the augmented matrix,
    write the equations in \gap{standard} \gap{form}.
    \begin{itemize}[nosep]
      \item $x$'s first, $y$'s second, $z$'s third 
      \item constant numbers on the right
    \end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\myWideProblemWithContent[Write the augmented matrix for these systems of equations and state its dimensions.]
{
    \vspace{-1\onelineskip}
    \begin{flalign*}
        &2x = z + 13& \\ 
        &x + y + z = -7& \\
        &-3z = 22 + y&
    \end{flalign*}
}[\large]
