\section{Checking for Factors}

A linear binomial \gap{$(x-a)$} is a \gap{factor} of a polynomial, $P(x)$, 
if the \gap{remainder} of
{\( \frac{P(x)}{x-a} \) }
is \gap{zero}.

\myWideProblemWithContent[Is $(x-1)$ a factor of this polynomial? Explain why or why not.]
{
    \centering
    $
        9x^3 - 18x + 9
    $
    \tcblower
    \mySyntheticDivisionScaffold{4}
}
%[\whenTEACHER{Yes b/c the remainder is zero.}]
