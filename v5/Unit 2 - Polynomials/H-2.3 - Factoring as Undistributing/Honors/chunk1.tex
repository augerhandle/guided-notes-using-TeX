\begin{tcolorbox}[center,width=5.5in]
    These two mathematical operations are the ``reverse'' of each other:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \gap{distributing} short polynomials into a long one
        \[
            (x + 2)(x+3)  \text{\LARGE\,\,$\Rightarrow$\,\,}  x^2 + 5x + 6
        \]
        \item \gap{factoring} a long polynomial into simple ones
        \[
            (x + 2)(x+3)  \text{\LARGE\,\,$\Leftarrow$\,\,}  x^2 + 5x + 6
        \]
    \end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

In this lesson, we will \gap{factor} \gap{polynomials} 
given a long-ish list of polynomial \gap{distribution} \gap{facts}.
