
\section{Differences of Perfect Squares}

Polynomials are {\bfseries\itshape classified} by the \gap{number} of {\bfseries\itshape terms} 
separated by addition or subtraction.

\begin{myCenteredBox}[colback=white,]
    \large
    \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1}
    \begin{tabular}{r|ll}
        \gap{monomial} 
            & {\bfseries\itshape one} term by itself
            & $5x^4$ \\
        \gap{binomial} 
            & {\bfseries\itshape two} terms 
            & $3x^2 - 1$ \\
            \gap{trinomial} 
            & {\bfseries\itshape three} terms 
            & $-7x^2 + 5x + 6$ \\
        \gap{polynomial} 
            & {\bfseries\itshape any number} of terms 
            & $5x^4 - x^3 + 3x^2 -x + 2$ \\
    \end{tabular}    
\end{myCenteredBox}

Polynomials are also {\bfseries\itshape classified} by \gap{degree},
the largest \gap{exponent} on the variable.
