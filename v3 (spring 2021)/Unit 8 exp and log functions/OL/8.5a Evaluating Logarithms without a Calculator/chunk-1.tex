\section{Evaluating Logarithms Without a Calculator}

Calculators have \gap{logarithm} buttons.
But we don't always need them.

\begin{myConceptSteps}{%
    ~To evaluate logarithmic expressions without a calculator\dots}
    \myStep{$y$}{Prepend $y=$ before the expression to write it in \gap{logarithmic} form.}
    \myStep{rewrite}{Rewrite that equation in \gap{exponential} form.}
    \myStep{exponent}{Find the exponent that makes the equation true.}
\end{myConceptSteps}

\begin{my2Problems}{1.25in}[Evaluate these logarithms without a calculator.]
    {$log_5(125)$}
    {$log_6(\frac{1}{36})$}
\end{my2Problems}
\begin{my2Problems}{1.25in}
    {$log_{5}3125$}
    {$log_4{\frac{1}{1024}}$}
\end{my2Problems}
