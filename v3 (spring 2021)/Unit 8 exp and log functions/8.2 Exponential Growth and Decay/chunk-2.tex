\section{Negative exponents}

Suppose \gap{$b>1$}.
Then $f(x) = b^x$ is exponential \gap{growth}.
What about $g(x) = \left(\frac{1}{b}\right)^x$?

If $b>1$, 
then \gap{$0<\frac{1}{b}<1$}.
So $g(x)$ is exponential \gap{decay}.
We can rewrite $g$ as $g(x) = b^{-x}$. 

\begin{myCenteredBox}[width=4in,]
    So
    if $b>1$,
    \[   g(x) = b^{-x}   \]
    is another way to write 
    {\bfseries\itshape exponential decay} functions.
\end{myCenteredBox}



\begin{myCenteredBox}[width=4in,]
    In general,\par
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{c||c|c}
        {} & {\bfseries\itshape growth} & {\bfseries\itshape  decay} \\
        \hline
        $y=b^x$ &  $b>1$ & $0<b<1$ \\
        $y=b^{rx}$
            & 
            $b>1$, \quad $r>0$
            &
            $b>1$, \quad $r<0$
            \\
    \end{tabular}
\end{myCenteredBox}
