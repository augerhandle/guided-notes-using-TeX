\section{The Quadratic Formula}

Solve quadratic equations in {\bfseries\itshape standard form} 
\gap{$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$}
using this formula:

\myCenteredBox[
    width=6.5in,
    title={The Quadratic Formula},
    colbacktitle=blue!10!white,
    coltitle=black,
    ]{
    {
    \large
    \[
        x = \frac 
                {-b \pm \myRoot{b^2 - 4ac}}
                {2a}
    \]
    {\normalsize which is really \gap{two} solutions:}
    \begin{align*} 
        x &= \frac 
        {-b \bm{+} \myRoot{b^2 - 4ac}}
        {2a}
        &
        x &= \frac 
        {-b \bm{-} \myRoot{b^2 - 4ac}}
        {2a}
    \end{align*}
    }
}

\hfill 
\begin{minipage}{0.4\textwidth}
The expression $b^2-4ac$ determines {how many} solutions there are.
\end{minipage}
\hfill 
\begin{minipage}{0.55\textwidth}
\begin{center}
    % \small
    \begin{tabular}{cl}
        \toprule
        $D = b^2-4ac$ & \# solutions \\
        \midrule
        $D > 0$ & two solutions \\
        $D = 0$ & one solution \\
        $D < 0$ & no {\bfseries\itshape real} solutions \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{minipage}
