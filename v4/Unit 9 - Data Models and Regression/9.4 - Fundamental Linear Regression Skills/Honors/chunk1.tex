\section{Creating a table from a scatterplot}

\begin{tcolorbox}[center,width=5.5in]
    \small
    Before you can do linear regression, you need a \gap{table} of $x-y$ values. 
    If you only have a scatterplot, you must 
    \begin{itemize}[nosep]
        \item Find the $x$--$y$ coordinates of each point.
        \item Put the coordinates of each point into a table.
    \end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\vspace{-1\onelineskip}
\myWideProblemWithContent[Convert this scatterplot to an $(x,y)$ table.]
{
    \small
    %
    \begin{myTikzpictureGrid}{0.4}[-1]{6}[-1]{6}[solid]
    \end{myTikzpictureGrid}
    % %
    \hfill 
    % % %
    \begin{tabular}[b]{c|c}
        \toprule 
        $x$ & $y$ \\ 
        \midrule 
        \hspace{0.5in} & \hspace{0.5in} \\
        \, & \, \\
        \, & \, \\
        \, & \, \\
        \, & \, \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
}
